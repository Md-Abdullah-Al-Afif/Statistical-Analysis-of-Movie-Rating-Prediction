## Project Overview

This project investigates the factors that influence IMDb movie ratings using the **IMDb Popular Movies with TMDB Enrichment Dataset**. Through statistical methods and probability theory, the study identifies significant relationships between movie ratings, audience engagement, movie duration, budget, and revenue.

The analysis combines descriptive statistics, probability theory, Bayesian reasoning, probability distributions, hypothesis testing, correlation analysis, regression modeling, and data visualization to uncover meaningful insights into movie quality, popularity, and audience behavior.

## Key Findings

- Audience engagement (**Votes**) and **Duration** were identified as the strongest predictors of IMDb ratings.
- Movies with above-median vote counts showed a significantly higher probability of receiving high ratings.
- High-rated movies were found to have longer average durations than the overall movie population.
- Production **Budget** showed almost no direct relationship with IMDb ratings.
- Budget and Revenue demonstrated a strong positive correlation, indicating that higher investments often lead to greater commercial returns.
- Regression analysis confirmed that Votes and Duration contribute significantly to rating prediction.
- Probability analysis and Bayesian reasoning revealed a strong relationship between audience engagement and perceived movie quality.
- Central Limit Theorem simulations and hypothesis testing validated the reliability of the statistical conclusions.
